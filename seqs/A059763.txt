%N A059763 Primes starting a Cunningham chain of the first kind of length 4.
%C A059763 Initial (unsafe) primes of Cunningham chains of first type with length exactly 4. Primes in A059453 which survive as primes just three "2p+1 iterations", forming chains of exactly 4 terms.
%C A059763 The definition indicates each chain is exactly 4 primes long (i.e. the chain cannot be a subchain of a longer one). That's why this sequence is different from A023272 which gives also primes included in longer chains ("starting" them or not).
%H A059763 Chris Caldwell's Prime Glossary, <a href="http://primes.utm.edu/glossary/page.php?sort=CunninghamChain">Cunningham chains</a>.
%F A059763 {(p-1)/2, p, 2p+1, 4p+3, 8p+7, 16p+15} = {composite, prime, prime, prime, prime, composite}.
%e A059763 1229 is here because, through 2p+1, 1229 -> 2459 -> 4919 -> 9839 and the chain ends here since 2*9839 + 1 = 11*1789 is composite.
