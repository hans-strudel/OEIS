%N A010925 Pisot sequence T(5,21), a(n)=[ a(n-1)^2/a(n-2) ].
%C A010925 Different from A019992. The two sequences differ from n=26 on ( A010925(26) = 70795240417122019 <> 70795240417122020 = A019992(26)). From Boyd's paper in Advances in Number Theory, T(5,21) satisfies the rational generating function F(x)/(1+x-x*F(x)), with F(x)=5+x-x^2-x^4-x^26-x^2048, a 2048-order recurrence; and not the A019992 generating function: F(x)/(1+x-x*F(x)), with F(x)=5+x-x^2-x^4, which gives the 5-order recurrence for A019992. This and other examples show that it is essential to reject conjectured generating functions for Pisot sequences until a proof or reference is provided. - Pab Ter (pabrlos(AT)yahoo.com), May 23 2004
