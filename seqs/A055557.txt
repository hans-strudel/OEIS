%N A055557 Numbers n such that 3*R_n - 2 is prime, where R_n = 11...1 is the repunit (A002275) of length n.
%C A055557 Also numbers n such that (10^n-7)/3 is prime.
%C A055557 Sierpiński attributes the primes for n = 2,...,8 to A. Makowski.
%C A055557 The history of the discovery of these numbers may be as follows: a(1)-a(7), Makowski; a(8)-a(18), Caldwell; a(19), Earls; a(20)-a(31), Kamada. (Corrections to this account will be welcomed.)
%C A055557 Concerning certifying primes, see the references by Goldwasser et al., Atkin et al. and Morain. - Labos
%C A055557 No more than 14 consecutive exponents can provide primes because for exponents 15k+2, 16k+9, 18k+12, 22k+21, a(n)'s are divisible by 31, 17, 19, 23 respectively. Here 7 of possible 14 is realized. - _Labos Elemer_, Jan 19 2005
