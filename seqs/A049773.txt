%N A049773 Triangular array T read by rows: if row n is r(1),...,r(m), then row n+1 is 2r(1)-1,...,2r(m)-1,2r(1),...,2r(m).
%C A049773 n-th row = (r(1),r(2),...,r(m)), where m=2^(n-1), satisfies r(r(k))=k for k=1,2,...,m and has exactly 2^[ n/2 ] solutions of r(k)=k. (The function r(k) reverses bits. Or rather, r(k)=revbits(k-1)+1.
%C A049773 In a knockout competition with m players, arranging the competition brackets (see links) in r(k) order, where k is the rank of the player, ensures that highest ranked players cannot meet until the later stages of the competition.  None of the top 2^p ranked players can meet earlier than the p-th from last round of the competition.  At the same time the top ranked players in each match meet the highest ranked player possible consistent with this rule.  The sequence for the top ranked players meeting the lowest ranked player possible is A208569.  See also A131271. - _Colin Hall_, Jul 31 2011, Feb 29 2012
