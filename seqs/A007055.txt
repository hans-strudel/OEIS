%N A007055 Let S denote the palindromes in the language {0,1}*; a(n) = number of words of length n in the language SS.
%C A007055 Number of words in {0,1}* of length n that are rotations of their reversals. - _David W. Wilson_, Jan 01 2012
%D A007055 N. J. A. Sloane and Simon Plouffe, The Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences, Academic Press, 1995 (includes this sequence).
%H A007055 Chuan Guo, J. Shallit, A. M. Shur, <a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/1503.09112">On the Combinatorics of Palindromes and Antipalindromes</a>, arXiv preprint arXiv:1503.09112 [cs.FL], 2015.
%H A007055 R. Kemp, <a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/0012-365X(82)90123-6">On the number of words in the language {w in Sigma* | w = w^R }^2</a>, Discrete Math., 40 (1982), 225-234.
%F A007055 a(n) = A187272(n) - Sum_{d|n,d<n} phi(n/d)*a(d). - _Andrew Howroyd_, Mar 29 2016
%e A007055 S = {e, 0, 1, 11, 101, 111, 1001, 1111, 10001, 10101, 11011, 11111, 100001, ...}, where e is the empty word.
%e A007055 SS contains all words in {0,1}* of length <= 5, but at length 6 is missing the 12 words { 001011, 001101, 010011, 010110, 011001, 011010, 100101, 100110, 101001, 101100, 110010, 110100 }.
%e A007055 In more detail:  All words in SS of length 6 have one of the following 6 patterns: abccba, abbacc, aabccb, abcbad, abacdc, abcdcb. This gives a total of 3*(2^3+2^4) = 72 = A187272(n) words with some words being counted multiple times as follows:(x6): 000000, 111111(x3): 010101, 101010(x2): 001001, 010010, 011011, 100100, 101101, 110110These are exactly the repetitions of shorter words in SS. Subtracting gives a(6) = 72-5*2-2*2-1*6 = 52.
%e A007055 For length n=7:  All words in SS of length 7 have one of the following 7 patterns: abcdcba, abccbad, abcbadd, abbacdc, abacddc, aabcdcb, abcddcb.This gives a total of 7*2^4 = 112 = A187272(n) words with some words being counted multiply times.In particular, the words 0000000 and 1111111 are counted 7 times each so a(7)=112-6*2=100. - Information about examples courtesy of _Andrew Howroyd_, Mar 30 2016
