%N A056888 a(n) = number of k such that sum of digits of 9^k is 9n.
%C A056888 Proposed by _Mark Sapir_, Math. Dept., Vanderbilt University, who remarks (August 2000) that he can prove that a(n) is always finite and that a(1) = 2.
%C A056888 Values of a(n) for n>1 computed numerically by _Michael Kleber_, Sep 02 2000 and _David W. Wilson_, Sep 06 2000.
%C A056888 All terms except the first are only conjectures. For the theorem that a(n) is always finite, see Senge-Straus and Stewart. - _N. J. A. Sloane_, Jan 06 2011
%D A056888 H. G. Senge and E. G. Straus, PV-numbers and sets of multiplicity, Periodica Math. Hungar., 3 (1971), 93-100.
%D A056888 C. L. Stewart, On the representation of an integer in two different bases, J. Reine Angew. Math., 319 (1980), 63-72.
%e A056888 There are two powers of 9 with digit-sum 9, namely 9 and 81, so a(1) = 2.
