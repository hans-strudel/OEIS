%C A000111 For n >= 2, a(n-2) = number of permutations w of an ordered n-set {x_1 < ... x_n} with the following properties: w(1)=x_n, w(n)=x_{n-1}, w(2)>w(n-1), and neither any subword of w, nor its reversal, has the first three properties. The count is unchanged if the third condition is replaced with w(2)<w(n-1). - _Jeremy L. Martin_, Mar 26 2010
