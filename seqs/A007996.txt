%N A007996 Primes that divide at least one term of the sequence f given by f(1) = 2, f(n+1) = f(n)^2-f(n)+1 = A000058(n).
%C A007996 Or, let S_1 = [2] and let S_{n+1} = list formed by sorting the union of S_n together with all prime factors of 1 + Product_i S_n(i) into increasing order; sequence is limit as n -> infinity of S_n.
%C A007996 Prime divisors of the terms of Sylvester's sequence A000058. - _Max Alekseyev_, Jan 03 2004. Also of A007018. - _N. J. A. Sloane_, Jan 27 2007
%C A007996 Because all terms of the sequence f(n) are coprime, a prime can divide at most one term. Odoni shows that primes p>3 in this sequence must satisfy p=1 (mod 6). - _T. D. Noe_, Sep 25 2010
%C A007996 See A180871(n) for the index of the first term of A000058 (this is one less than the index of the f-sequence) divisible by a(n). - _M. F. Hasler_, Apr 24 2014
