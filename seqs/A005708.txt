%N A005708 a(n) = a(n-1) + a(n-6), with a(i) = 1 for i = 0..5.
%C A005708 This comment covers a family of sequences which satisfy a recurrence of the form a(n) = a(n-1) + a(n-m), with a(n) = 1 for n = 0...m-1. The generating function is 1/(1-x-x^m). Also a(n) = sum_{i=0..n/m} binomial(n-(m-1)*i, i). This family of binomial summations or recurrences gives the number of ways to cover (without overlapping) a linear lattice of n sites with molecules that are m sites wide. Special case: m=1: A000079; m=4: A003269; m=5: A003520; m=6: A005708; m=7: A005709; m=8: A005710.
%C A005708 For n>=6, a(n-6) = number of compositions of n in which each part is >=6. - _Milan Janjic_, Jun 28 2010
%C A005708 Number of compositions of n into parts 1 and 6. - _Joerg Arndt_, Jun 24 2011
%C A005708 The compositions of n in which each natural number is colored by one of p different colors are called p-colored compositions of n. For n>=6, 2*a(n-6) equals the number of 2-colored compositions of n with all parts >=6, such that no adjacent parts have the same color. - _Milan Janjic_, Nov 27 2011
%C A005708 a(n+5) equals the number of binary words of length n having at least 5 zeros between every two successive ones. - _Milan Janjic_, Feb 07 2015
