%N A058032 Largest m such that 2^n / primorial(m) >= 1.
%C A058032 Primorial order of powers of 2.
%F A058032 a(n)=Max{s| q(s)=Sign[Floor[2^n/A002110(n)]]=1}
%e A058032 for n=1 and 2, when 2 and 4 is divided by 2 gives quotient = 1 or 2, but when divided by 6 q<1, so the largest suitable primorial is the first; thus a(1)=a(2)=1. n=11, 2^11=2048. The largest primorial P, such that 2048/P > 1 is P=210, the 4th = A002110(4). So a(11)=4.
