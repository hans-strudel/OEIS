%N A056172 Number of non-unitary prime divisors of n!.
%C A056172 A non-unitary prime divisor for n! cannot exceed n/2. a(n)=PrimePi[n/2]
%F A056172 A prime divisor of x is non-unitary iff its exponent is at least 2 in the prime power factorization of x. In general, GCD[p, x/p]=1 or p. Cases are counted when GCD[p, n/p]>1.
%e A056172 10!=2*2*2*2*2*2*2*2*3*3*3*3*5*5*7. The non-unitary prime divisors are 2, 3, and 5 because their exponents exceed 1, so a(10)=3.  The only unitary prime divisor of 10! is 7.
