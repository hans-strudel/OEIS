%N A024447 Sum of the products of the primes taken 2 at a time from first n primes.
%C A024447 a(n) = 2nd elementary symmetric function of the first n+1 primes.
%C A024447 Using the identity that (x_1 + x_2 + ... + x_n)^2 - (x_1^2 + x_ 2^2 + ... + x_n^2) is the sum of the products taken two at a time, a(n) can be expressed with the sum of the primes and the sum of the prime squared. Since they both have asymptotic formulas, this yields an asymptotic formula for this series. - _Timothy Varghese_, May 06 2014
%H A024447 Robert Israel, <a href="/A024447/b024447.txt">Table of n, a(n) for n = 1..10000</a>
%F A024447 a(1) = 0, a(n+1) = prime(n+1)*(sum of first n primes) + a(n), for n > 1.
%F A024447 a(n) = ((A007504(n))^2 - A024450(n)))/2. - _Timothy Varghese_, May 06 2014
%F A024447 a(n) ~ (3*n^4*log^2(n) - 4*n^3*log^2(n))/24. - _Timothy Varghese_, May 06 2014
