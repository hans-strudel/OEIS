%C A061395 a(n) = the largest part of the partition having Heinz number n. We define the Heinz number of a partition p = [p_1, p_2, ..., p_r] as Product(p_j-th prime, j=1...r) (concept used by Alois P. Heinz in A215366 as an "encoding" of a partition). For example, for the partition [1, 1, 2, 4, 10] we get 2*2*3*7*29 = 2436. Example: a(20) = 3; indeed, the partition having Heinz number 20 = 2*2*5 is [1,1,3]. - _Emeric Deutsch_, Jun 04 2015
