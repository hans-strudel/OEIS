%N A059261 Hilbert's Hamiltonian walk on N X N projected onto the first diagonal: M(3) (sum of the sequences A059252 and A059253).
%C A059261 The interest comes from a simplest recursion than the cross-recursion, dependent on parity, governing the projections onto the x and y axis.
%H A059261 A. Karttunen, <a href="/A059261/b059261.txt">Table of n, a(n) for n = 0..65535</a>
%F A059261 Initially, M(0)=0; recursion: M(n+1)=M(n).f(M(n), n).f(M(n), n+1).d(M(n), n); -f(m, n) is the alphabetic morphism i := i+2^n; [example: f(0 1 2 1 2 3 4 3 4 5 6 5 4 3 2 3, 2)=4 5 6 5 6 7 8 7 8 9 10 9 8 7 6 7 ] -d(m, n) is the complementation to 2^(n-1)*3-2, alphabetic morphism; [example: d(0 1 2 1 2 3 4 3 4 5 6 5 4 3 2 3, 3)=10 9 8 9 8 7 6 7 6 5 4 5 6 7 8 7] Here is M(3). [M(1)=0.1.2.1, M(2)=0 1 2 1.2 3 4 3.4 5 6 5.4 3 2 3]
