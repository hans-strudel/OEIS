%N A025142 Fixed point of the square runlength transform, with a(1) = 1 (the runlength transform of a binary sequence is the sequence of the lengths of its runs).
%C A025142 Unique sequence a such that a(1) = 1 and RL^2(a) = a != RL(a), where for any sequence s of 1's and 2's, the runlength transform of s RL(s) is such that RL(s)(n) = length of n-th run of same symbols in s; RL(a) is sequence A025143.
%C A025142 Conjecture: as for the Oldenburger-Kolakoski sequence A000002, which is a fixed point of RL, it remains to be shown that the limiting frequency of 1's and 2's in this sequence is 0.5. - _Jean-Christophe Hervé_, Oct 21 2014
%C A025142 Like the Oldenburger-Kolakoski sequence, this sequence is cubefree and for this reason some words can never appear like XYXYX. Also, the number of 1's and 2's in any word of length 10 is 4, 5 or 6 (see comments in A000002). - _Jean-Christophe Hervé_, Oct 21 2014

%e A025142 We illustrate how this sequence and A024143 can be constructed from each other. Start with two 1's in this sequence: a(1) = a(2) = 1 (and thus a(3) = 2), which gives A025143(1) = 2 (first run length of this sequence), followed by a 1 because a(1) = 1 is also the first run length of A025143; thus a(4) = 1, which gives A024143(3) = 2, which in turn gives a(5) = 1 and a(6) = 2, etc. - _Jean-Christophe Hervé_, Oct 21 2014
