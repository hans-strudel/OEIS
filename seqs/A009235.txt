%F A009235 Recurrence equation: a(n+1) = sum {k = 0..n} (-2)^(n-k)*C(n,k)*a(k). Written umbrally this is a(n+1) = (a-2)^n (expand the binomial and replace a^k with a(k)). More generally, a*f(a) = f(a-2) holds umbrally for any polynomial f(x). An inductive argument then establishes the umbral recurrence a*(a+2)*(a+4)*...*(a+2*(n-1)) = 1 with a(0) = 1. Cf. A004211.
%F A009235 Touchard's congruence holds for odd prime p: a(p+k) = (a(k) + a(k+1)) (mod p) for k = 0,1,2, ... (adapt the proof of Theorem 10.1 in Gessel). In particular, a(p) = 2 (mod p) for odd prime p.
