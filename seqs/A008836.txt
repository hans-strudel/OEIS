%C A008836 Coons and Borwein: "We give a new proof of Fatou's theorem: if an algebraic function has a power series expansion with bounded integer coefficients, then it must be a rational function.} This result is applied to show that for any non--trivial completely multiplicative function from N to {-1,1), the series sum_{n=1 to infinity) f(n)z^n is transcendental over {Z}[z]; in particular, sum_{n=1 to infinity) lambda(n)z^n is transcendental, where lambda is Liouville's function. The transcendence of sum_{n=1 to infinity) mu(n)z^n is also proved." - _Jonathan Vos Post_, Jun 11 2008
