%F A000048 a(n) = (1/(2*n)) * Sum_{odd d divides n} mu(d)*2^(n/d), where mu is the Mobius function A008683.
%F A000048 a(n) = A056303(n) for all integer n>=2. - _Michael Somos_, Dec 20 2014
%F A000048 Sum_{k dividing m for which m/k is odd} k*a(k) = 2^(m-1). (This explains the observation that the sequence is very close to A006788. Unless m has some nontrivial odd divisors that are small relative to m, the term m*a(m) will dominate the sum. Thus, we see for instance that a(n) = A006788(n) when n has one of the forms 2^m or 2^m*p where p is an odd prime with a(2^m) < p.) - _Barry R. Smith_, Oct 24 2015
