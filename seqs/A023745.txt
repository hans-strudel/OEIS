%N A023745 Plaindromes: numbers whose digits in base 3 are in nondecreasing order.
%H A023745 Harvey P. Dale, <a href="/A023745/b023745.txt">Table of n, a(n) for n = 1..1000</a>
%F A023745 Numbers that in ternary are the concatenation of i 1's with j 2's, i, j>=0. Also a(n) = A073216(n+1) - 1. Proof: Write a(n) as 1{m}2{n}, then adding 1 gives 1{m-1}20{n} for m>0 and 10{n} for m=0. Doubling yields 10{m-1}10{n} or 20{n}, respectively. These two forms exactly describe the forms of sums of two powers of 3, the two powers being 3^n and 3^(m+n). - Hugo van der Sanden.
%e A023745 In base 3 these numbers are 0, 1, 2, 11, 12, 111, 112, 122, 222, 1111, 1112, ...
