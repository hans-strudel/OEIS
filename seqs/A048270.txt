%N A048270 Sequence of 2 Pythagorean triangles, each with a leg and hypotenuse prime. The leg of the second triangle is the hypotenuse of the first.
%C A048270 It is conjectured that there is an infinite number of such pairs of triangles.
%C A048270 Subsequence of A048161. - _Lekraj Beedassy_, Sep 16 2005
%H A048270 H. Dubner and T. Forbes, <a href="https://cs.uwaterloo.ca/journals/JIS/VOL4/DUBNER/pyth.html">Prime Pythagorean triangles</a>, J. Integer Seqs., Vol. 4 (2001), #01.2.3.
%F A048270 For each p(n), there is a q=(p*p+1)/2 and r=(q*q+1)/2 such that p, q, r, are all prime.
%e A048270 P(1)=3 because 3 is prime, 5=(3*3+1)/2 and 13=(5*5+1)/2, 5,13 both prime.
