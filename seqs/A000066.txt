%S A000066 4,6,10,14,24,30,58,70,112,126
%N A000066 Smallest number of vertices in trivalent graph with girth (shortest cycle) = n.
%C A000066 Also called the order of the (3,n) cage graph.
%C A000066 Recently (unpublished) McKay and Myrvold proved that the minimal graph on 112 vertices is unique. - May 20 2003
%C A000066 If there are n vertices and e edges, then 3n=2e, so n is always even.
%C A000066 Current lower bounds for a(13)..a(32) are 202, 258, 384, 512, 768, 1024, 1536, 2048, 3072, 4096, 6144, 8192, 12288, 16384, 24576, 32768, 49152, 65536, 98304, 131072. - from Table 3 of the Dynamic cage survey via _Jason Kimberley_, Dec 31 2012
%C A000066 Current upper bounds for a(13)..a(32) are 272, 384, 620, 960, 2176, 2560, 4324, 5376, 16028, 16206, 49326, 49608, 108906, 109200, 285852, 415104, 1141484, 1143408, 3649794, 3650304. - from Table 3 of the Dynamic cage survey via _Jason Kimberley_, Dec 31 2012
