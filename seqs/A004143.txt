%N A004143 From the powers that be.
%C A004143 For n>0, let b be the smallest nonnegative integer such that 2^m_1 > 3^m_2 > ... > prime(n)^m_n, where m_i is the exponent satisfying prime(i)^m_i <= b < prime(i)^(m_i+1). This sequence records the exponent m_1 for b because b=2^m_1. - _Tom Edgar_, Dec 05 2014
%D A004143 N. J. A. Sloane and Simon Plouffe, The Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences, Academic Press, 1995 (includes this sequence).
