%N A046711 From the Bruck-Ryser theorem: numbers n == 1 or 2 (mod 4) which are also the sum of 2 squares.
%C A046711 If n = 1, 2 (mod 4) and the squarefree part of n is divisible by a prime p = 3 (mod 4), then no difference set of order n exists. Equivalently, if a projective plane of order n exists, and n= 1 or 2 (mod 4), then n is the sum of two squares. - _Jonathan Vos Post_, Apr 17 2011
