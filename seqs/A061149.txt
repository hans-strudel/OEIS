%N A061149 Smallest number whose number of divisors = n-th primorial (A002110).
%C A061149 The n-th term is divisible by the product of first n primorial numbers (A006939(n)), the n-th Chernoff-number. Also the numbers are refactorable (A033950).
%C A061149 The formula computes a(n) correctly. [From _T. D. Noe_, May 17 2010]
%F A061149 The n-th term is constructed as a product of special powers of the first n prime, as follows: a(n)=Product[ p(j)^[p(n-j+1)-1] ], j=1...n
%e A061149 a(1)=2 a(2)=(2^2)*(3^1)=12, a(3)=(2^4)*(3^2)*(5^1)=720 .... a(7)=(2^16)*(3^12)*(5^10)*(7^6)*(11^4)*(13^2)*(17^1)= 1683176415906545239680000000000. a(7) is divisible with the product of the first 7 primorial numbers(=A006939(7)): a(7)/2677277333530800000=628689600000.
