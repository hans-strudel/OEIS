%N A060598 Number of 7-dimensional tilings of unary zonotopes. The zonotope Z(D,d) is the projection of the D-dimensional hypercube onto the d-dimensional space and the tiles are the projections of the d-dimensional faces of the hypercube. Here d=7 and D varies.
%D A060598 A. Bjorner, M. Las Vergnas, B. Sturmfels, N. White and G.M. Ziegler, Oriented Matroids, Encyclopedia of Mathematics 46 Second Edition, Cambridge University Press, 1999
%D A060598 N. Destainville, R. Mosseri and F. Bailly, Fixed-boundary octagonal random tilings: a combinatorial approach, Journal of Statistical Physics, 102 (2001), no. 1-2, 147-190.
%D A060598 Victor Reiner, The generalized Baues problem, in New Perspectives in Algebraic Combinatorics (Berkeley, CA, 1996-1997), 293-336, Math. Sci. Res. Inst. Publ., 38, Cambridge Univ. Press, Cambridge, 1999.
%H A060598 M. Latapy, <a href="http://www.liafa.jussieu.fr/~latapy/Zono/index.html">Tilings of Zonotopes</a>
%e A060598 For any d, the only possible tile for Z(d,d) is Z(d,d) itself, therefore the first term of the series is 1. It is well known that there are always two d-tilings of Z(d+1,d), therefore the second term is 2. More examples are available on my web page.
