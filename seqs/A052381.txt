%N A052381 The smallest initial prime of 2 non-overlapping d-twin primes if the distance between pairs (D) is minimal (see A052380).
%C A052381 A prime quadruple (triple), {[p,p+d],[p+D,p+D+d]} is called "non-overlapping" (disjoint or touching) pair of twins if D=distance>=d=difference "inside" twin.
%F A052381 Smallest p so that [p, p+2n], [p+min{D}, p+2n+min{D}] is a quadruple (or triple if d=min{D}) of consecutive primes
%e A052381 If n=23, d=46, min{D}=48 then the first suitable quadruple of primes is [15370423, 15370469, 15370471, 15370517] with [46, 2, 46] difference pattern; if n=3, d=6, min{D}=6 then the first such triple is [47, 53, 53, 59]=[47, 53, 59] with [6, 6] difference pattern.
