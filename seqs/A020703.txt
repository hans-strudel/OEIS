%C A020703 The sequence may also be defined as follows: a(1)=1, a(n)=m^2 (where m^2 is the least square > a(k) for 1<=k<n), if the minimal natural number not encountered so far is greater than a(n-1), else a(n)=a(n-1)-1.
%C A020703 A reordering of the natural numbers.
%C A020703 The sequence is self-inverse in that a(a(n))=n.
%C A020703 (End)

%F A020703 a(n)=a(n-1)-1, if a(n-1)-1 > 0 is not in the set {a(k)| 1<=k<n}, else a(n)=m^2, where m^2 is the least square not encountered so far.
%F A020703 a(n)=n for n=k(k+1)+1, k>=0.
%F A020703 a(n+1)=(sqrt(a(n)-1)+2)^2, if a(n)-1 is a square, a(n+1)=a(n)-1, else.
%F A020703 a(n)=2*(floor(sqrt(n-1))+1)*floor(sqrt(n-1))-n+2. (End)
%e A020703 a(2)=4=2^2, since 2^2 is the least square >2=a(1) and the minimal number not encountered so far is 2>1=a(1);
%e A020703 a(8)=6=a(7)-1, since the minimal number not encountered so far (=5) is <=7=a(7).
