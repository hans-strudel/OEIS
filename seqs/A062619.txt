%N A062619 Numbers n such that 53^n - 52^n is a prime.
%C A062619 Terms greater than 1000 often correspond only to strong pseudoprimes 53^n-52^n. [Clarified by _M. F. Hasler_, Sep 15 2013]
%C A062619 All terms are prime. - _Robert Price_, Jan 20 2013
%C A062619 a(12) > 10^5 - _Robert Price_, Jan 20 2013
%C A062619 To elaborate on Robert Price's comment that all terms are prime: This is also true for all other sequences of numbers n such that k^n - (k - 1)^n is prime. It is easy to prove by elementary means that if n = a * b, then k^n - (k - 1)^n is divisible by k^(n - a) - (k - 1)^(n - a) and k^(n - b) - (k - 1)^(n - b). - _Alonso del Arte_, Sep 15 2013
