%N A050412 Riesel problem: start with n; repeatedly double and add 1 until reaching a prime. Sequence gives number of steps to reach a prime or 0 if no prime is ever reached.
%C A050412 a(n) = smallest m >= 1 such that (n+1)*2^m - 1 is prime (or 0 if no such prime exists).
%C A050412 It is conjectured that n = 509203 is the smallest Riesel number, i.e., n*2^k -1 is composite for every k>0. - _Robert G. Wilson v_, Mar 01 2015
%H A050412 T. D. Noe and Robert G. Wilson v, <a href="/A050412/b050412.txt">Table of n, a(n) for n = 1..2291</a> First 657 terms from T. D. Noe.
%H A050412 Ray Ballinger and Wilfrid Keller, <a href="http://www.prothsearch.net/rieselprob.html">The Riesel Problem: Definition and Status</a>, Proth Search Page.
%e A050412 For n=4; the smallest m>=1 such that (4+1)*2^m-1 = prime is m=2: 5*2^2-1=19 (prime). - _Jaroslav Krizek_, Feb 13 2011
