%N A005044 Alcuin's sequence: expansion of x^3/((1-x^2)*(1-x^3)*(1-x^4)).
%C A005044 a(n) = number of triangles with integer sides and perimeter n.
%C A005044 Also a(n) = number of triangles with distinct integer sides and perimeter n+6, i.e., number of triples (a, b, c) such that 1 < a < b < c < a+b, a+b+c = n+6. - _Roger Cuculière_.
%C A005044 With a different offset (i.e., without the three leading zeros, as in A266755), the number of ways in which n empty casks, n casks half-full of wine and n full casks can be distributed to 3 persons in such a way that each one gets the same number of casks and the same amount of wine [Alcuin]. E.g., for n=2 one can give 2 people one full and one empty and the 3rd gets two half-full. (Comment corrected by _Franklin T. Adams-Watters_, Oct 23 2006)
