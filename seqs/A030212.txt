%C A030212 Called chi_4(n) by Glaisher and Hardy because as Glaisher (1907) writes on page 21 "It can be shown (see section 53) that chi_4(n) admits of an arithmetical definition, being in fact equal to one-fourth of the sum of the fourth powers of all complex numbers which have n as norm, viz. chi_4(n) = 1/4 sum_n (a + i b)^4, where a + i b is any number which has n for norm. It is in consequence of this definition that the notation chi_4(n) has been used." - _Michael Somos_, Jun 18 2012
