%N A052130 a(n) = number of numbers between 1 and 2^m with m-n prime factors (counted with multiplicity), for m sufficiently large.
%C A052130 a(n) = number of products of half-odd-primes <= 2^n. E.g., a(2) = 7 since 1, 3/2, (3/2)^2, (3/2)^3, (3/2)*(5/2), 5/2, 7/2 are all <= 2^2. - _David W. Wilson_
%C A052130 m is sufficiently large precisely when 2^m > 3^(m-n), i.e., when m >= floor(n*log(3)/log(1.5)) = A117630(n+1) = A126281(n) for n > 1. (Robert G. Wilson v asks if this conjecture holds in a comment to A126281.) - _David A. Corneth_, Apr 09 2015
%H A052130 <a href="/index/Pri#primepop">Index entries for sequences related to numbers of primes in various ranges</a>
%e A052130 Between 1 and 2^m there is just one number with m prime factors, namely 2^m, so a(0) = 1.
%e A052130 For m >= 3, up to 2^m there are 2 numbers with m-1 prime factors, 2^(m-1) and 3*2^(m-2), so a(1) = 2.
