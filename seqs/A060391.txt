%N A060391 If 10^n can be written as x*y where the digits of x and y are all nonzero, then let a(n) = largest such y, otherwise a(n) = -1.
%C A060391 According to Ogilvy and Anderson, 10^33 is the highest known power of ten that can be expressed as the product of two zero-free factors. "If there is another one, it is greater than 10^5000." p. 89
%D A060391 C. Stanley Ogilvy and John T. Anderson, Excursions in Number Theory, Oxford University Press, 1966, p. 89.
%D A060391 Rudolph Ondrejka, Nonzero factors of 10^n, Recreational Mathematics Magazine, no. 6 (1961), p. 59.
%e A060391 10^2 = 4 * 25, so a(2) = 25.
