%N A046001 Maximal number of ordinary double points on an n-th degree algebraic surface in complex projective 3-space.
%C A046001 For n >= 7 lower bounds are 99, 168, 226, 345, 425, 600, 732, 949; upper bounds are 104, 174, 246, 360, 480, 645, 829, 1051. The bounds on mu(d), the maximum number of singularities on a surface of degree d in three-space, are 5/12*d^3 <= mu(d) <= 4/9*d^3. - Corrected and extended by _Martin Renner_, Nov 10 2014
%D A046001 S. Endrass, Flaechen mit vielen Doppelpunkten. DMV-Mitteilungen 4 (April 1995), 17-20.
%H A046001 S. Endrass, <a href="http://enriques.mathematik.uni-mainz.de/kon/docs/Eflaechen.shtml">Surfaces with many ordinary nodes</a>
%H A046001 Oliver Labs, <a href="http://www.oliverlabs.net/view.php?menuitem=160">Nodal surfaces</a>
%H A046001 Eric Weisstein's World of Mathematics, <a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/OrdinaryDoublePoint.html">Ordinary Double Point</a>
%e A046001 For n = 3 there is a unique surface of degree 3 with 4 double points, Cayley's cubic: 4(w^3+x^3+y^3+z^3) = (w+x+y+z)^3.
