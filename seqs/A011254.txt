%N A011254 Numbers n such that phi(n) + sigma(n) = 4n.
%C A011254 If (sigma(m)-phi(m))/(4*m-sigma(m)-phi(m)) is a prime integer p not dividing m, then p*m is in the sequence. 135230346701100 is in the sequence and not divisible by 24. [From _Jens Kruse Andersen_, Feb 17 2009]
%C A011254 If n=80*m is in the sequence and gcd(m,10) = 1 then 200*m is also in the sequence. Proof: phi(200*m)+sigma(200*m) = phi(200)*phi(m)+sigma(200)*sigma(m) = 80*phi(m)+465*sigma(n) = 5/2*(32*phi(m)+186*sigma(m)) = 5/2*(phi(80)*phi(m)+sigma(80)*sigma(m)) = 5/2*(phi(80*m)+sigma(80*m)) = 5/2*(phi(n)+sigma(n)) = 5/2*(4*n)=5/2*(4*80*m) = 4*(200*m) so 200*m is in the sequence. [From _Farideh Firoozbakht_, Mar 30 2009]
