%C A057711 a(n) = number of permutations on [n] for which the entries of each left factor form a circular subinterval of [n]. A subset I of [n] forms a circular subinterval of [n] if it is an ordinary interval [a,b] or has the form [1,a]-union-[b,n] for 1<=a<b<=n. For example, (5,4,2) is a left factor of the permutation (5,4,2,1,3) which does not form a circular subinterval of [5] and a(4)=16 counts all 24 permutations of [4] except the eight whose first two entries are 1,3 (in either order) or 2,4. - _David Callan_, Mar 30 2007
