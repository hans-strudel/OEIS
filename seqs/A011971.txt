%N A011971 Aitken's array: triangle of numbers {a(n,k), n >= 0, 0<=k<=n} read by rows, defined by a(0,0)=1, a(n,0)=a(n-1,n-1), a(n,k)=a(n,k-1)+a(n-1,k-1).
%C A011971 Also called the Bell triangle or the Peirce triangle.
%C A011971 a(n,k) is the number of equivalence relations on {0, ..., n} such that k is not equivalent to n, k+1 is not equivalent to n, ..., n-1 is not equivalent to n. - _Don Knuth_, Sep 21 2002 [Comment revised by Thijs van Ommen (thijsvanommen(AT)gmail.com), Jul 13 2008]
