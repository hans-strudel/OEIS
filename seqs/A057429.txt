%N A057429 Numbers n such that (1+i)^n - 1 times its conjugate is prime.
%C A057429 Equivalently, numbers n such that (1+i)^n - 1 is a Gaussian prime.
%C A057429 Note that n must be a rational prime. Also note that (1+i)^n + i or (1+i)^n - i is also a Gaussian prime. - _T. D. Noe_, Jan 31 2005
%C A057429 Primes which are the norms of the Gaussian integers (1 + i)^n - 1 or (1 - i)^n - 1. - _Jonathan Vos Post_, Feb 05 2010
%C A057429 Let z = (1+i)^n - 1. The product of z and its conjugate is 1 + 2^n - cos(n*Pi/4)*2^(1+n/2). For n > 3, the primes are in A007670 or A007671 depending on whether n = {1, 7} (mod 8) or n = {3, 5} (mod 8), respectively. - _T. D. Noe_, Mar 07 2010
%C A057429 Primes p such that ((1+i)^p - 1)((1-i)^p - 1) is prime. Number 2 together with odd primes p such that the norm 2^p - (-1)^((p^2-1)/8)*2^((p+1)/2) + 1 is prime. Note that Legendre symbol (2/p) = (-1)^((p^2-1)/8) as above. - _Thomas Ordowski_, Feb 20 2013

%C A057429 The primes generated by these series are also generalized unique primes. They can be represented as Phi(4, 2^((p+1)/2) - (2/p))/2, where (2/p) is the Legendre symbol (Cf. link to Generalized unique primes page at UTM). - _Serge Batalov_, Sep 08 2014
