%C A006092 The next 179 terms are all the numbers from 221 to 399. Assuming that '1000' is spelled 'one thousand', the next terms are the numbers in the intervals [x*1000 , x*1000+999] where x runs over all elements a(1),..,a(225) occuring up to there. Then occur all numbers in the intervals [x*10^6, (x+1)*10^6-1], where x again runs over all preceding terms.
%D A006092 Archimedeans Problems Drive, Eureka, 48 (1988), 56.
%D A006092 N. J. A. Sloane and Simon Plouffe, The Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences, Academic Press, 1995 (includes this sequence).
%F A006092 Let g_k(x) = (x^(2k) - x^(4k) + x^(10k) - x^(11k) + x^(12k) - x^(14k) + x^(20k) - x^(40k) + x^(200k) - x^(400k))/(1-x). Then the g.f. of the characteristic function of this sequence is sum(j>=0, g_{1000^j}(x).
