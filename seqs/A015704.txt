%N A015704 a(n) is the smallest number m such that phi(m) + sigma(m) = n*m.
%C A015704 The offset is 2, because for all numbers m, phi(m)+sigma(m) >= 2*m, so there is no number a(1) such that phi(a(1))+sigma(a(1))=1*a(1). - Farideh Firoozbakht
%C A015704 a(5) >= 2*10^9. - Farideh Firoozbakht
%C A015704 10^13 < a(5) <= 336280120525440. Charles R Greathouse IV showed that 6 divides a(5). 336280120525440 and 60493590969525342720 are the only m values I found such that phi(m) + sigma(m) = 5*m. - _Donovan Johnson_, Sep 11 2012
