%N A005360 Flimsy numbers.
%C A005360 Definition: a(n) is flimsy iff there exists a k such that A000120(k*n) < A000120(n). That is, some multiple of n has fewer ones in its binary expansion than does n. What are the associated k for each n? What is the smallest n for each k? Stolarsky says "at least half the primes are flimsy." - _Jonathan Vos Post_, Jul 07 2008
%C A005360 A143073(n) gives the least k for each n in this sequence. - _T. D. Noe_, Jul 22 2008
