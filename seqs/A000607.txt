%F A000607 From _Vaclav Kotesovec_, Sep 15 2014: (Start)
%F A000607 It is surprising that the ratio of the formula for log(a(n)) to the approximation 2 * Pi * sqrt(n/(3*log(n))) exceeds 1. For n=20000 the ratio is 1.00953, and for n=50000 (using the value from Havermann's tables) the ratio is 1.02458, so the ratio is increasing. See graph above.
%F A000607 A more refined asymptotic formula is proved by Vaughan in Ramanujan J. 15 (2008), p. 109-121: log(a(n)) = 2*Pi*sqrt(n/(3*log(n))) * (1 + log(log(n))/log(n) + O(1/log(n)))

%t A000607 a[n_] := a[n] = If[PrimeQ[n], 1, 0];
%t A000607 c[n_] := c[n] = Plus @@ Map[# a[#] &, Divisors[n]];
