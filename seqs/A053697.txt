%N A053697 a(n+1)=a(n)+a^(n), where the addition is in base 11 and where a^(n) is obtained from a(n) by replacing each digit by its multiplicative inverse modulo 11. Zero digits, if any, are deleted.
%C A053697 Conjecture. For any positive integer a(1), the sequence generated according to the above rule eventually cycles through the forms a(k)=[1][4^a][3][(84)^b],..., a(k+6)=[1][4^a][3][(84)^(b+1)], or through a(k)=[1][5^a][4][(84)^b],..., a(k+6)=[1][5^a][4][(84)^(b+1)], for nonnegative integers a and b. The sequence listed above, with a(1)=1, is an example of the first type.
