%C A004146 a(n) is also the number of points of Arnold's "cat map" that are on orbits of period n-1. This is a map of the two-torus T^2 into itself. If we regard T^2 as R^2 / Z^2, the action of this map on a two vector in R^2 is multiplication by the unit-determinant matrix A = [2, 1;1, 1], with the vector components taken modulo one. As such, an explicit formula for the n-th entry of this sequence is -det(I-A^n). - _Bruce Boghosian_, Apr 26 2009
