%C A023000 6*a(n) =: z(n) gives the approximation up to 7^n for one of the three 7-adic integers (-1)^(1/3), i.e. z(n)^3 + 1 == 0 (mod 7^n), n>=0, and z(n) == 6 (mod 7) == -1 (mod 7), n>=1. The companion sequences are x(n)=A210852(n) and y(n)=A212153(n). This leads to a(n) == 1 (mod 7) for n>=1 (this is also clear from some of the formulae given below). Also 216*a(n)^3 + 1 == 0 (mod 7^n), n>=0, as well as 3*216*a(n)^2 + A212156(n) == 0 (mod 7^n), n>=0. a(n) = 6^(7^(n-1)-1) (mod 7^n), n>=1. A recurrence is a(n) = a(n-1) + 7^(n-1), with a(0)=0, for n>=1.
%C A023000   Also a(n) = (1/6)*(6*a(n-1))^7 (mod 7^n) with a(1)=1 for n>=1. Finally, 6^3*a(n-1)*a(n)^2 + 1 == 0 (mod 7^(n-1)), n>=1.
