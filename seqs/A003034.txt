%N A003034 Sylvester's problem: minimal number of ordinary lines through n points in the plane.
%C A003034 An ordinary line contains exactly 2 points. The problem is to place n points, not all on a line, so as to minimize the number of ordinary lines.
%C A003034 Pach and Sharir give the following table for n >= 3: 3, 3, 4, 3, 3, 4, 6, 5, 6, 6, 6, 7, ?, 8, ?, 9, ?, ?, ?, 22, ... - _N. J. A. Sloane_, Dec 01 2010
