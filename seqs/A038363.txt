%N A038363 Two different numbers a, b which satisfy sigma(a)=sigma(b)=(a+b)^3/(a^2+b^2) are called a rational amicable pair; sequence gives b numbers.
%C A038363 These are just the known values. There is no proof that this sequence is correct (in the usual sense that there are no other terms below the last term shown). - _N. J. A. Sloane_, Nov 07 2006
%C A038363 Factorization: 2^(n-1)*M(n)*3*5^2*13*31*139*277*3877*239 where M(n) is Mersenne Prime, n=3 or 5<n.
