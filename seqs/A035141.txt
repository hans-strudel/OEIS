%F A035141 a(n) ~ n. Proof: the density of numbers without a given decimal digit in their prime factors is 0, which can be seen by looking at first (or second, in the case of 0) digit and removing all primes with that digit. Taken with the 0 density of numbers missing any decimal digit the result is obtained. - _Charles R Greathouse IV_, May 02 2013
