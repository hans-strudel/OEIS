%N A006039 Primitive non-deficient numbers.
%C A006039 A number n is non-deficient (A023196) iff it is abundant or perfect, that is iff A001065(n) is >= n. Since any multiple of a non-deficient number is itself non-deficient, we call a non-deficient number primitive iff all its proper divisors are deficient. - Jeppe Stig Nielsen (mail(AT)jeppesn.dk), Nov 23 2003
