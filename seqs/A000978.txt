%N A000978 Wagstaff numbers: numbers n such that (2^n + 1)/3 is prime.
%C A000978 It is easy to see that the definition implies that n must be an odd prime. - _N. J. A. Sloane_, Oct 06 2006
%C A000978 The terms from a(31) on only give probable primes. Caldwell lists the largest certified primes. - _Jens Kruse Andersen_, Jan 11 2006
%C A000978 Prime numbers of the form 1+Sum_{i=1..m} [2^(2i-1)]. - _Artur Jasinski_, Feb 09 2007
%C A000978 There is a new conjecture stating that a Wagstaff number is prime under the following condition (based on DiGraph cycles under the LLT): Let p be a prime integer > 3, N(p) = 2^p+1 and W(p) = N(p)/3, S(0) = 3/2 (or 1/4) and S(i+1) = S(i)^2 - 2 (mod N(p)). Then W(p) is prime iff S(p-1) == S(0) (mod W(p)). - Tony Reix (tony.reix(AT)laposte.net), Sep 03 2007
%C A000978 As a member of the DUR team (Diepeveen, Underwood, Reix), and thanks to the LLR tool built by Jean Penne, I've found a new and big Wagstaff PRP: (2^4031399+1)/3 is Vrba-Reix PRP! This Wagstaff number has 1,213,572 digits and today is the 3rd biggest PRP ever found. I've done a second verification on a Nehalem core with the PFGW tool. - Tony Reix (tony.reix(AT)laposte.net), Feb 20 2010
