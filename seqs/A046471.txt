%N A046471 Number of numbers k>1 such that k equals the sum of digits in k^n.
%C A046471 The number of digits in k^n is at most 1+n*log(k). Hence the maximum sum of digits of k^n is 9(1+n*log(k)). By solving k=9(1+n*log(k)), we can compute an upper bound on k for each n. Sequence A133509 lists the n for which a(n)=0.
