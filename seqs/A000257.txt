%F A000257 Integral representation as the n-th moment of the positive function W(x) on (0,8). In Maple notation, a(n) = int(x^n*W(x), x=0..8), n=1,2,3,... , where W(x)=sqrt((8-x)^3/x)/(32*Pi). For n=0 the integral is equal 3/4. This means that a(n) is the n-th moment, n=0,1,2,... , of probability distribution which is a sum of W(x) as the continuous part and an atom at x=0 with weight 1/4 (Dirac(x)/4). This representation is unique as W(x) is the solution of the Hausdorff moment problem. - _Karol A. Penson_ and Wojciech Mlotkowski, Jul 15 2015
