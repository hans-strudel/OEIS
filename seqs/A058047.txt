%S A058047 3,5,7,29,41,79
%N A058047 Generalized Collatz sequences: primes resulting in a cycle containing 1.
%C A058047 For each prime P check the generalized Collatz sequence of each integer N > 1 defined by c(1) = N, c(n+1) = c(n) * P + 1 if F > P, else c(n+1) = c(n) / F, where F is the smallest factor of c(n), until c(n) = c(m) for n > m starts a cycle. If all c(i) > 1, then P does not belong to the sequence (and vice versa).
%C A058047 All terms are as yet only conjectures. _Jeff Heleen_ checked the primes < 1000 and start points up to 10000000 (see Prime Puzzle 114 and example below). a(1)=3 is the ordinary Collatz problem. - Frank Ellermann, Jan 20 2002.

%e A058047 For p = 29 e.g.: 17, 17*29+1=494, 494/(2*13*19)=1, 1*29+1=30, 30/30=1 (cycle with 1), no counter-example below 10000000.
