%C A008287 T(n,k) is the number of compositions of k into n parts p, each part 0<=p<=3. Adding 1 to each part, as a corollary, T(n,k) is the number of compositions of n+k into n parts p where 1<=p<=4. E.g., T(2,3)=4 since 3=0+3=3+0=1+2=2+1. In general, the entry (n,k) of the (l+1)-nomial triangle gives the number of compositions of k into n parts p, each part 0<=p<=l. - _Steffen Eger_, Jun 18 2011
