%C A027907 T(n,k) = number of integer strings s(0),...,s(n) such that s(0)=0, s(n)=k, s(i)=s(i-1)+c, where c is 0, 1 or 2. Columns of T include A002426, A005717 and A014531.
%C A027907 Also number of ordered trees having n+1 leaves, all at level three and n+k+3 edges. Example: T(3,5)=3 because we have three ordered trees with 4 leaves, all at level three and 11 edges: the root r has three children; from one of these children two paths of length two are hanging (i.e., 3 possibilities) while from each of the other two children one path of length two is hanging. Diagonal sums are the tribonacci numbers; more precisely: Sum(T(n-i,i), i=0..floor(2n/3)) = A000073(n+2). - _Emeric Deutsch_, Jan 03 2004

%C A027907 The trinomial coefficients, T(n,i), are the absolute value of the coefficients of the chromatic polynomial of P_2 X P_n factored with x(x-1)^i terms. Example: The chromatic polynomial of P_2xP_2 is: x(x-1) - 2x(x-1)^2 + x(x-1)^3 and so T(1,0)=1, T(1,1)=2 and T(1,1)=1. - Thomas J Pfaff (tpfaff(AT)ithaca.edu), Oct 02 2006

%C A027907 T(n,k) is the number of compositions of k into n parts p, each part 0<=p<=2. Adding 1 to each part, as a corollary, T(n,k) is the number of compositions of n+k into n parts p where 1<=p<=3. E.g., T(2,3)=2 since 5=3+2=2+3. - _Steffen Eger_, Jun 10 2011
