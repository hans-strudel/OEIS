%N A054979 e-perfect numbers: numbers n such that the sum of the e-divisors (exponential divisors) of n equals 2n.
%C A054979 The e-divisors (or exponential divisors) of x=Product p(i)^r(i) are all numbers of the form Product p(i)^s(i) where s(i) divides r(i) for all i.
%C A054979 The number of e-divisors for n = Product[p_i^e_i] is given by Product[tau(e_i)] (A000005). - _Jon Perry_, Nov 13 2012
%C A054979 Conjecture: Every e-perfect number is divisible by 36. - _Jon Perry_, Nov 13 2012
%D A054979 R. K. Guy, Unsolved Problems In Number Theory, B17.

%e A054979 The e-divisors of 36 are 2*3, 4*3, 2*9 and 4*9 and the sum of these = 2*36, so 36 is e-perfect.
