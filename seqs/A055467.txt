%N A055467 Composite numbers for which Sum of EulerPhi and Divisor-Sum is an integer multiple of the cube of the number of divisors.
%C A055467 Makowski proved that phi(n)+Sigma[n] = nd[n] iff n is a prime (see in Sivaramakrishnan,Chapter I, page 8, Theorem 3) In more special cases k differs from n and Phi+Sigma is divisible with higher powers of the number of divisors
%D A055467 Sivaramakrishnan,R.(1989):Classical Theory of Arithmetical Functions Marcel Dekker,Inc., New York-Basel.
%F A055467 Integer solutions of Phi[x]+Sigma[x] = kd[x]^3 or A000203(n)+A000010(n) = k*A000005(n)^3, where k is integer.
%e A055467 n = 95 with 4 divisors,Sigma(95) = 120, Phi(95) = 72 72+120 = 192 = 3*4*4*4, k = 3
