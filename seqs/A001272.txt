%N A001272 Numbers n such that n! - (n-1)! + (n-2)! - (n-3)! + ... - (-1)^n*1! is prime.
%C A001272 At present the terms >= 2653 are only probable primes.
%C A001272 Zivkovic shows that all terms must be less than p=3612703, which divides a(n) for n>=p. - _T. D. Noe_, Jan 25 2008
%C A001272 Notwithstanding Zivkovic's wording, p=3612703 also divides the alternating factorial for n=3612702. [Guy: If there is a value of n such that n+1 divides af(n), then n+1 will divide af(m) for all m>n.] Therefore af(3612701), approximately 7.3*10^22122513, is the final primality candidate. - _Hans Havermann_, Jun 17 2013
